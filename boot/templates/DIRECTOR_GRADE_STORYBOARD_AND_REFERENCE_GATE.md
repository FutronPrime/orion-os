# ORION Director-Grade Storyboard and Visual-Reference Gate

Portable, system-agnostic acceptance contract.

## Production order

Research and rights → rendered references → Story Bible Matrix/state ledger → spatial package →
director board → human approval → generation/animation → edit/finish → release approval.

## Visual-reference minimum

1. Character-only neutral turnaround: front, three-quarter, profile, back; head/hair/hands/expression.
2. Isolated wardrobe, footwear, jewelry, grooming and story-state variants.
3. Multi-view functional and damaged-state sheets for props, vehicles, devices and creatures.
4. Environment visual plate plus separate architectural floor/roof/section/elevation drawings.
5. Separate civil/GIS-style street map with roads, sidewalks, parcels, footprints, entrances,
   north arrow, scale, landmarks/camera anchors and route.
6. Stable IDs/versions, approval, provenance, checksums, QA, and downstream attachment receipts.

Descriptions and scene crops cannot satisfy these roles.

## Director-board minimum

Shared creative choices; actual approved visual locks; spatial proof; distinct story panels; per-shot
duration, lens, framing, height, angle, movement, blocking, action, audio, transition, entry/exit
state and narrative purpose; palette/lighting/grade; technique-based cinematic lineage; continuity;
cost/rights/safety; approval status.

## Deterministic test

Fail when any required role lacks an approved file, declared dimensions, matching checksum,
continuity test, matrix binding or attachment manifest. Missing billing receipts remain `UNKNOWN`,
never free. Reference approval never implies animation, publication or payment approval.
